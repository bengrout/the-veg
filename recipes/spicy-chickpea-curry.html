Spicy chickpea curry

Ingredients for 10 servings

  • 3 onions
  • 5 cans of chickpeas
  • 3 cans of chopped tomatoes
  • 1.5 bulbs of garlic
  • 2.5 thumb sized pieces of ginger
  • 5 mixed peppers
  • 17.5g ground coriander (2.5tbsp)
  • 35g ground cumin (5tbsp)
  • 17.5g garam masala (2.5tbsp)
  • 15.5g turmeric (2.5tbsp)
  • 80g tomato puree (5tbsp)
  • 1.5 packs of fresh coriander
  • 1.5 lemons
  • 3 green chillis (more if you like it hot)
  • 500g basmati rice or 5 cups
  • Cooking oil or ghee

Method

Prep

  • Get two large pots of water on the boil - one for rice and one for chickpeas
  • Dice the onions
  • Chop peppers into large chunks
  • Measure out spices and mix together
  • Peel and chop the ginger (big bits is ok)
  • Chop the chillis (big bits is ok)
  • Peel the ginger
  • Drain the tinned chickpeas
  • Chop the coriander (we use it near the end)
  • Squeeze the lemons (we use it near the end)

Prepare the chickpeas

  • Bring the chickpeas to the boil in a large pan of salted water.
  • Once boiling, remove from the heat and set aside. Don’t drain them yet.

Making the paste

  • Fry the onions until soft (5 to 10 minutes)
  • Whizz In the food processor until it is a paste:
    • Garlic
    • Ginger
    • Spice mix
    • Tomato pureé
    • Chillis (don’t add to mild curry)
    • A splash of oil

Cook it

  • Heat the cooking oil in a deep pan
  • Fry the paste for 5 minutes on a medium heat. Keep stirring so it doesn’t burn. Add a splash of chickpea water if it is sticking or too dry.
  • Add some of the chickpea water to the pot (scoop with an empty can - half a can for 20 servings, a full can for 40)
  • Drain the chickpeas and add to the paste (keep a little water spare), along with the tinned tomatoes. Simmer for 5 - 10 minutes until reduced
  • While simmering, boil the rice
  • Add half the coriander and the lemon juice to the pot and put the lid on
  • Serve with a little extra coriander on top
